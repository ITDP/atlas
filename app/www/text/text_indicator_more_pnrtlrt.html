People Near Rapid Transport

Rapid public transport includes all forms of public transport that run at high speeds in their own separated tracks or ways â€“ heavy rail / metro trains, light rail, and bus rapid transit. Because rapid transport bypasses traffic congestion, it can be the fastest way to move around a city. Rapid transport can carry more people, farther and faster, than any other mode, and becomes increasingly necessary as a city grows.

However, rapid transport can never serve every neighborhood in a city, and so other modes - including frequent transport, walking, and bicycling - are necessary for shorter trips or trips in less dense areas.

HOW IT'S CALCULATED

We use data from the Transit Explorer to identify the locations of rapid transport stations, and use the street network from OpenStreetMap to count the number of people living within a 1km walk of those stations.

If you notice an error in the data representing your city, or if your city is not included in the Transit Explorer, please use the interface on that page to submit a correction, or contact data@itdp.org.

Policy Recommendations
  • Extend existing rapid transit lines or build new ones in areas where the most people live. This will have a more impactful result than building lines in less densely-populated areas.
  • Build more housing near rapid transport stations, to make more efficient use of existing infrastructure.
  • Develop long-term plans for rapid transport, which usually takes years to build.
  • Follow the Bus Rapid Transit Standard to build effective high-quality rapid transit for a much lower cost than metro rail.
