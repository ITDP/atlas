Predecessors

The Atlas builds on nearly a decade of work by ITDP’s many offices to develop meaningful indicators of sustainable mobility and to operationalize them for transport planning. These include:

  1. Best Practice in National Support for Urban Transportation (2014), which introduced the concept of measuring the ratio of a country’s rapid transport network to the number of urban residents.
  2. People Near Transit (2016), which introduced the measurement of the percentage of a city’s population within walking distance of rapid transport, with support from the Ford Foundation and the William and Flora Hewlett Foundation.
  3. Indicators of Sustainable Mobility (2019), which measured People Near Rapid Transport and other indicators in North American cities, with support from the Toyota Mobility Foundation.
  4. Pedestrians First (2019), which introduced the measurement of Block Density.
  5. Pedestrians First Online Tool (2020), which provided measurements of Block Density, Population Density, People Near Services, and People Near Frequent Transport worldwide. Both versions of Pedestrians First were supported by the Bernard van Leer Foundation.
  6. MobiliDADOS , which provides measurements of People Near Rapid Transport and People Near Bikeways, among other indicators, in Brazil. It provides more granular analysis than the Atlas, and includes demographic breakdowns of indicator values. It was developed with support from the Instituto Clima e Sociedade and the Oak Foundation.
  7. The Rapid Transit Database , which tracks BRT, LRT, and metro lines worldwide. Since it is not geospatial, we have used the Transit Explorer instead, but we have cross-referenced the Transit Explorer with the RTDB to validate data.
